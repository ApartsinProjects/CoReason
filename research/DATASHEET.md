# Datasheet: CoReasoning controlled-generation dataset

A datasheet (after Gebru et al., 2021) for the data released with the CoReasoning paper.

## Motivation
The data supports a *feasibility demonstration* that the Framing/Judging/Steering instrument
discriminates competence and that the three skills dissociate. It is **not** a learning-outcomes
dataset and contains **no human-subject data**.

## Composition
- **Challenges** (`research/data/llm_cache/01-*,02-*,03-*,14-*`): LLM-generated ill-defined problems,
  three per-skill rubrics, gold "best framing", and a seeded-flaw AI solution (with internal_issues),
  across subjects: algorithms, microeconomics, machine learning, databases, statistics.
- **Simulated learners**: responses generated at controlled per-skill competence (`expert`/`novice`)
  by `gpt-4o-mini` (`sim_*` cache). Judging responses are programmatic selections over ground-truth
  seeded issues. These are **apparatus, not people**.
- **Grades**:
  - `results/e3_dissociation_grades.csv` — 40 learners (8 crossed per-skill profiles × 5 challenges),
    each with per-skill competence level and A/B/C grade. Grader: `gpt-4o`.
  - `results/e2_reliability.csv` — grader test-retest (N=5 repeats per cell, 4 profiles).
  - `results/e1_construct_validity.json` — inter-skill correlations + factor summary.
- **Instrument**: the 16 production prompts at `code/artifacts/prompt-debug/originals/*.yaml`.

## Collection process
Fully synthetic and reproducible. Generated by the harness in `research/scripts/` over the
Groq/OpenRouter/OpenAI APIs. Deterministic per-call disk cache (`research/data/llm_cache/`) makes the
exact model outputs reproducible without re-calling the APIs. Seeds are derived from
`stable_seed(challenge, levels)`.

## Preprocessing / labeling
Grades are produced by the rubric-driven evaluator+grader prompts (08/09/10 → 11). No human labels are
included. The accompanying human-rater validation package (`human-study/`) is the protocol by which
human labels *would* be collected; it has not been run.

## Uses
Intended: reproducing the feasibility analyses; stress-testing the grader; building on the instrument.
Out of scope: any claim about real student learning, or treating simulated grades as ground truth for
human competence.

## Limitations
Single grader model family; English-only; small N (feasibility scale); the deployed prototype's
original *response* data was synthetic seed placeholders and is therefore excluded. See paper §11.
