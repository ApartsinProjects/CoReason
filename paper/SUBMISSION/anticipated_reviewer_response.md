# Anticipated Reviewer Concerns and How the Paper Addresses Them

A self-prepared map of the concerns a rigorous reviewer (education-sciences or computing-in-education
lens) is likely to raise, and where each is handled. Intended as a head start on the eventual
response-to-reviewers; it also documents the design decisions for transparency.

| # | Anticipated concern | How the paper addresses it |
|---|---------------------|----------------------------|
| 1 | "The three skills are just a relabeling of prompting / not really distinct." | The dissociation experiment (Section 8): a crossed factorial over **N=80** simulated learners shows each skill's grade tracks its *own* manipulated competence while staying flat in the others (mean cross-effect +0.01). The cleanest demonstration is **Framing-Judging ρ=-0.03 (ns)**, two skills graded by separate mechanisms yet uncorrelated, with a multitrait-multimethod analysis (below) giving the convergent side. |
| 1b | **"The dissociation is true by construction: you injected independent competences and then recovered independence (a manipulation check, not a finding)."** | Two analyses show the instrument is *faithful*, not hardwired to dissociate (Section 8, convergent/discriminant validity). (i) On learners whose three competences share a single latent level, the **same instrument returns correlated grades** (Framing-Judging ρ=+0.52, mean ρ=+0.41) versus near-zero on the independent crossed design (mean \|ρ\|=0.13). It recovers independence when present and correlation when present. (ii) Treating the three grader backends as methods, the same skill agrees across vendors at the per-learner level (Framing ρ=+0.57, Judging ρ=+0.67), the convergent-high / discriminant-low pattern a valid measure must show (Campbell & Fiske, 1959). The crossed-design separation is therefore a property of the skills, not of the scoring. |
| 2 | "Novelty vs 4D AI Fluency / Tankelevitch (2024) / prompt-literacy." | Section 6 + Table 1 map each prior framework onto the triad and show that none separates pre-generation Framing from post-generation Steering *and* treats all three as independently scored. The temporal separation is the wedge. |
| 3 | "It is an LLM-as-judge; the grades are not validated against humans." | We label self-consistency as a *precision, not accuracy* property (Section 8), report it (≈92%), and present a fully prepared human-rater agreement study (codebook, blinded task files, κ/α scoring) in Section 10, framed via Messick/Kane validity (scoring + generalization supported; extrapolation + implication pending). |
| 4 | "The 'dissociation' headline is inflated by Judging's perfect +2.00." | The headline is the two blind-graded effects, Framing (+0.62) and Steering (+0.43), with CIs excluding zero; the pooled diagonal is not reported as a headline number. Judging's +2.00 is fixed by construction (programmatic selection) and read as a controlled check that the grader rewards recall and precision. |
| 5 | "The single grader model could be the source of the effect." | Grader-backend robustness across vendors (Section 8): the dissociation replicates across **three backends spanning two providers** (gpt-4o, gpt-4o-mini, and Meta's llama-3.3-70b; cross-skill ratio 39 under the independent vendor). Judging's own-effect settles to +0.65/+0.75 under the two non-gpt-4o graders, level with Framing, so the separation is balanced across skills and version-independent. |
| 6 | "Steering's own-effect is weak; maybe the construct is not real." | A harsher-steering ablation (Section 8) tested the leniency hypothesis directly: a strict rubric did **not** raise the effect (+0.40 vs +0.50), so the weak signal is not a rubric artifact. We attribute it to the P2 ceiling confound (steering bounded by judging, now visible as a significant J-S correlation at N=80) and simulated-response homogeneity, both resolvable with human data. |
| 7 | "The paper describes a 'deployed platform' but the figures look like mockups." | Section 7 discloses that the interface figures are representative mockups of the prototype, not screenshots of a running deployment, and that the released, evaluated artifact is the scoring engine (sixteen prompts) plus the harness. |
| 8 | "Theory is decorative / Nelson-Narens is misapplied (the AI is not the learner's own cognition)." | Section 3.1 frames the AI as an *external* object level, an explicit *exo-directed* extension of monitor/control, and derives the extra epistemic-vigilance burden (Proposition P4) from it. The learning-from-errors literature grounds the deliberately-imperfect-output design. |
| 9 | "Sample size / generalizability." | N=80 across **ten subjects**; own-effects positive in 9/10 subjects for Framing and Steering; bootstrap CIs reported. We are explicit that this is feasibility-scale and synthetic. |
| 10 | "Reproducibility." | All numbers recompute from released CSVs; the harness is deterministically cached; a datasheet documents the data. Two independent reviewers verified the numbers cell-by-cell during internal review. |
