## 1. Summary of the revision

The authors addressed the Round-2 must-dos in earnest. The substantive interpretive
items (weak-Steering reinterpretation with bootstrap CIs, proposition re-scoping, the
recursive grader-calibration threat, the Section 11 model-family contradiction) are all
handled cleanly, and three genuinely useful new analyses appear: a grader-reliability
panel (92% self-consistent, mean flip-rate 0.08), inter-skill Spearman correlations
across the 40 learners (F~J 0.00, F~S −0.22, J~S +0.17, all ns; first principal factor
42% of variance), and bootstrap 95% CIs on the diagonal own-effects. These materially
strengthen the dissociation argument: the near-zero, non-significant inter-skill
correlations plus a 42% first factor are now an independent line of evidence against the
single-"AI-use-ability" account, complementing the manipulation-based effect matrix.

One Tier-1 (blocking) defect from Round 2 was **not** fixed: the figure paths still point
at `assets/...` while the image files live only in `docs/assets/`. There is no
`paper/assets/` directory. This breaks all five figures, including the two central
evidentiary ones. Two cosmetic Minor items were also missed (the "60 to 1" ratio; the
255-call decomposition). These are the only things standing between the manuscript and
Accept.

**New additions — assessment:**

- **Reliability (lines 494–501).** Sound and honestly scoped. 92% self-consistency / mean flip-rate 0.08 (Judging 0.00 by construction, Framing 0.15, Steering 0.10), within-cell SD ≈ 0.2, on four held-fixed transcripts re-graded five times with cache bypassed. The text correctly frames this as *precision* (repeatability), not *accuracy* against humans, and ties it to the documented self-inconsistency of LLM judges. One caveat worth noting (optional, below): N=4 transcripts × 5 repeats is a thin base for a headline "92%," and Framing's 0.15 flip-rate is nearly double the reported mean — the aggregate slightly flatters the noisier skills.
- **Inter-skill Spearman + first factor (lines 469–473).** A real strengthening. Independent of the manipulation matrix, the near-zero non-significant correlations and 42% first-factor share argue against a single latent ability. Consistent with PROJECT_LOG C5. The "far below the near-100% a single 'AI-use ability' account would predict" framing is fair; the under-identification caveat (Section 8 caveat iii) keeps it from over-reaching into a factor-analytic claim.
- **Bootstrap CIs on the diagonal (lines 466–468).** Exactly what Round 1 and Round 2 asked for; both blind-graded own-effects exclude zero. Good.

## 3. Recommendation

**Recommendation: Minor Revision** (a hair from Accept; effectively *Accept with a
required production fix*).

Justification, calibrated to a selective venue. Four of the five Round-2 must-dos are
cleanly resolved, the fifth (figures) is resolved on numbering but not on paths, and the
three new analyses are honest and additive. The conceptual contribution, the novelty
boundary, and the blind-graded Framing/Steering dissociation evidence are all at the
level a Position Paper / Blue Sky track requires, and the scope discipline (no
learning-outcome claims; Judging diagonal flagged as partly built-in; human validation
prepared-not-run) is exemplary.

I cannot recommend outright Accept only because **the central evidentiary figures do not
render from the manuscript directory** (Section 2 below, blocking item). A selective venue
should not print a paper whose primary visual evidence for its central claim resolves to
five broken image links. This is a one-command build-hygiene fix with zero content or
compute implications, but it is genuinely blocking: were the figures path-correct, this
would be an Accept. I therefore recommend Minor Revision contingent solely on the single
blocking fix plus the two cosmetic Minors, with no further review cycle needed if the
editor verifies the rendered PDF/HTML shows all five figures.
