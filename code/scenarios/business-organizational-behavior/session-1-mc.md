## PHASE 1 — FRAMING

### Raw Problem Presented to Student

> Design a restructuring plan for a mid-size European pharmaceutical company (about 4,500 employees across R&D, manufacturing, and commercial) that just acquired a biotech startup of "around 200 people." The CEO wants to "preserve the startup's innovative culture" while "capturing synergies" in shared services. Several senior leaders from both organizations have "overlapping roles." Deliver an organizational chart for the integrated entity, a role rationalization framework, and a transition timeline. The board expects "quick wins within 90 days" but also wants a "sustainable long-term structure." Some startup employees have retention bonuses that expire "in about a year."

### Framing Options Presented

Which assumptions and constraints should you establish before the AI generates a solution?

- **(A)** Fully absorb the startup into the pharma company's existing structure from day one — assign all startup employees to existing departments, eliminate all overlapping roles immediately, and migrate to unified systems within 30 days
- **(B)** Assume the startup is R&D-focused, shared services synergies target finance/HR/IT, retention bonuses create a ~1-year integration window, overlapping roles number roughly 5-10 at VP+ level
- **(C)** Assume the startup is R&D-focused, question what specific cultural practices need structural protection (decision speed, flat hierarchy, risk tolerance), clarify decision rights for the semi-autonomous unit (budget, hiring, project authority), identify whether organizations are co-located or distributed, flag retention bonus timeline as a hard constraint
- **(D)** Keep both organizations completely separate with no integration — the startup should operate as a standalone subsidiary indefinitely with no shared services
- **(E)** Focus only on cost savings — eliminate all redundant roles in both organizations immediately and merge all functions within 60 days

**Student selects: (B)**

### AI Solution (Generated from Student's Framing)

**Organizational Chart:**
```
CEO (Pharma)
├── Chief Scientific Officer
│   ├── Pharma R&D (existing teams)
│   └── Biotech Innovation Unit (startup, semi-autonomous)
│       └── Reports dotted-line to CSO, solid-line to Startup Founder (Chief Innovation Officer)
├── Chief Commercial Officer (existing)
├── Chief Manufacturing Officer (existing)
├── Chief Financial Officer (consolidated)
│   ├── Pharma Finance
│   └── Startup Finance (absorbed)
├── CHRO (consolidated)
└── CIO (consolidated)
```

**Role Rationalization Framework:**
1. Map all VP+ roles across both entities
2. Classify as: Retain Both, Merge, Redeploy, Eliminate
3. Select based on competency assessment + strategic fit
4. Transition packages within 60 days

**Timeline:** Days 1-30: Announce structure. Days 31-60: Consolidate shared services. Days 61-90: Complete role rationalization. Months 4-12: Gradual R&D integration.

### Framing Feedback

**Criticism:** Option (B) is reasonable but incomplete. It correctly identifies the startup's likely R&D focus, the synergy targets, and the retention constraint. However, it does not question what "preserving innovative culture" means structurally — which specific practices need protection and how decision rights should be allocated. Option (C) was stronger because it probes the operational meaning of cultural preservation, clarifies the decision rights question that is central to any matrix or semi-autonomous structure, and asks about geographic distribution.

**Better Alternative:** Option **(C)** — it covers everything in (B) plus the critical questions about cultural specifics, decision authority, and co-location.

**Grade: B** — Solid practical framing, but missed the structural implications of the stated objectives.
