## PHASE 1 — FRAMING

### Raw Problem Presented to Student

> Design a crisis management strategy for a fictional U.S. National Security Council meeting following a naval confrontation between Chinese coast guard vessels and Philippine supply boats near a disputed reef in the South China Sea. A Philippine sailor was "seriously injured," and Manila is invoking its Mutual Defense Treaty with Washington. China claims the Philippines "provoked" the incident by entering "Chinese sovereign waters." Regional allies are "watching closely." Produce a decision memo for the National Security Advisor that recommends a course of action spanning the next 72 hours and a longer-term diplomatic strategy, including military posture adjustments, alliance signaling, and a proposed public statement.

### Student's Framing Response

> **Assumption: The confrontation is below the threshold of armed attack — coast guard vessels, not navy warships, were involved, so the Mutual Defense Treaty's application is legally ambiguous.**
>
> **Assumption: "Regional allies watching closely" means Japan, South Korea, Australia, and ASEAN states, each with different threat perceptions and alliance expectations.**
>
> **Constraint: The problem doesn't specify the current U.S. naval posture in the region — I'll assume a carrier strike group is within 48 hours of the area.**
>
> **Question: "Seriously injured" is vague — the severity affects the domestic political pressure on Manila and the proportionality calculus for any U.S. response.**

### AI Solution (Generated from Student's Framing)

**DECISION MEMO — FOR THE NATIONAL SECURITY ADVISOR**

**Subject:** South China Sea Incident Response

**Recommended 72-Hour Actions:**

1. **Diplomatic:** Issue a public statement condemning the use of force against civilian supply vessels. Call for immediate de-escalation. Request emergency consultations under ASEAN mechanisms.

2. **Military:** Reposition the USS Ronald Reagan carrier strike group to conduct a previously scheduled "freedom of navigation" transit within 200 nautical miles of the disputed reef. Increase P-8 Poseidon surveillance flights.

3. **Alliance:** Deploy the Deputy Secretary of State to Manila within 24 hours to demonstrate solidarity and coordinate a joint fact-finding team. Reaffirm the Mutual Defense Treaty applies to armed attacks on Philippine public vessels in the South China Sea.

4. **Public Statement:** "The United States stands with the Philippines. We condemn the dangerous and destabilizing actions by Chinese coast guard vessels. We call on all parties to resolve disputes peacefully consistent with international law, including the 2016 Arbitral Tribunal ruling."

**Longer-Term Strategy:**

- Propose a multilateral code of conduct negotiation framework through ASEAN
- Accelerate Philippine coast guard capacity-building assistance
- Coordinate with Japan and Australia for joint maritime patrols in the region

### Framing Feedback

**Criticism:** The student correctly identified the legal ambiguity of the treaty obligation (coast guard vs. navy) and the need to map regional allies' different interests — both strong analytical moves. However, the student did not question the **adversary's intent** — is this an isolated incident or part of a pattern of escalation? This distinction is critical for calibrating the response. The student also did not flag the **domestic political dimension** on the U.S. side (congressional pressure, election cycle) or the **informational environment** (social media, competing narratives). Finally, the student assumed a carrier strike group's proximity without questioning whether a military signal is the right first move.

**Better Alternative Framing:**
> "Assumption: This is a coast guard incident, not a military engagement — the MDT's applicability is legally debatable, which gives the U.S. both flexibility and vulnerability. Question: Is this incident isolated or part of an escalatory pattern? The response should differ significantly depending on the answer. Missing: What is the current state of U.S.-China diplomatic channels — is there a crisis hotline or recent high-level contact that could be activated? Missing: What are the domestic political constraints on both the U.S. and Philippine presidents that will shape their decision space?"

**Grade: A** — Strong on legal and alliance dimensions, but missed adversary intent analysis and domestic constraints.

**Better Alternative Steering:**
