## PHASE 1 — FRAMING

### Raw Problem Presented to Student

> Build an impact assessment for a proposed comprehensive trade agreement between the European Union and Mercosur (Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay, Paraguay). The deal would eliminate tariffs on "over 90% of goods" over a 15-year phase-in period, open services markets to "greater competition," and include a sustainability chapter with "binding commitments" on deforestation. European farmers warn of being "undercut by cheap imports," while Mercosur industrial sectors fear "European manufactured goods flooding the market." Environmental groups say the sustainability provisions are "toothless." Produce a structured assessment covering trade creation and diversion effects, distributional impacts across sectors, environmental and labor implications, and a set of recommendations for negotiators on both sides.

### Framing Options Presented

Which assumptions and constraints should you establish before the AI generates a solution?

- **(A)** Assume free trade is always beneficial. Focus the assessment on arguing for ratification. Ignore environmental concerns as outside the scope of a trade assessment.
- **(B)** Identify sensitive sectors likely excluded from the 90% (beef, ethanol for EU; autos, machinery for Mercosur). Request baseline tariff data. Question what "binding commitments" means operationally for deforestation. Note the 15-year phase-in signals significant expected adjustment costs.
- **(C)** Identify sensitive sectors and request baseline tariff data. Specify the analytical framework (static welfare vs. CGE model vs. political economy). Question non-tariff barriers (SPS standards, rules of origin, technical regulations) that often matter more than tariffs in EU agreements. Ask whether the sustainability chapter has trade sanctions, dispute settlement, or only consultative processes. Note that services liberalization may generate larger gains than goods trade but requires separate analysis.
- **(D)** Assume the agreement will fail because Mercosur countries are too protectionist. Focus on why EU farmers are right to oppose the deal.
- **(E)** The problem is clear enough — just ask the AI to write a balanced assessment.

**Student selects: (B)**

### AI Solution (Generated from Student's Framing)

**EU-MERCOSUR TRADE AGREEMENT: IMPACT ASSESSMENT**

**I. Trade Creation and Diversion Effects**

*Trade Creation:*
- EU exports to Mercosur: Machinery, automobiles, pharmaceuticals, chemicals (current Mercosur tariffs: 14-35%)
- Mercosur exports to EU: Beef, poultry, soybeans, ethanol, sugar (current EU tariffs: 12-40% + quotas)
- Estimated total new bilateral trade: significant but unquantified

*Trade Diversion:*
- Mercosur beef could displace Australian/New Zealand beef in EU
- EU autos could displace Japanese/Korean vehicles in Mercosur

**II. Distributional Impacts**
*EU Winners:* Industrial exporters
*EU Losers:* Farmers (beef, poultry, sugar) in France, Ireland, Poland
*Mercosur Winners:* Agricultural exporters
*Mercosur Losers:* Manufacturing sectors

**III. Environmental and Labor Implications**
Sustainability chapter labeled "binding" but enforceability unclear. Increased agricultural demand could drive deforestation.

**IV. Recommendations**
- EU: Insist on enforceable sustainability provisions; create transition funds for farmers
- Mercosur: Secure market access for beef/ethanol; protect infant industries with sunset clauses

### Framing Feedback

**Criticism:** Option (B) correctly identified the sensitive sectors, requested baseline tariff data, questioned the sustainability provisions, and noted the phase-in signal. However, it missed several critical dimensions that option (C) addressed. Option (C) additionally specified the analytical framework choice (which determines what the assessment can credibly claim), raised non-tariff barriers (often more impactful than tariffs), and noted that services liberalization may be the largest source of welfare gains. These are not minor additions — they shape the entire structure of the analysis.

**Better Alternative:** Option **(C)** — it provides a significantly more comprehensive foundation.

**Grade: B** — Solid baseline questions but missed the analytical framework, NTBs, and services dimension.
