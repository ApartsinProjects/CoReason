## PHASE 1 — FRAMING

### Raw Problem Presented to Student

> Design a blended professional development program to train 150 K-12 teachers across a school district in "technology-enhanced instruction." The program should run over one semester. Teachers range from "tech-savvy early adopters" to those who "still use overhead projectors." The district has a half-day per month reserved for PD but "teachers say it's never enough time." Some schools have strong Wi-Fi; others are "spotty at best." Deliver a semester-long PD calendar with the blended schedule, a facilitator guide for in-person sessions, design specifications for the online components, and a plan for measuring whether teachers actually change their classroom practice. The superintendent wants to "see results by spring."

### Student's Framing Response

> **Assumption: "Technology-enhanced instruction" means integrating digital tools (presentation software, LMS features, formative assessment apps like Kahoot or Nearpod) into existing classroom practice — not a complete pedagogical overhaul.**
>
> **Assumption: The semester runs January through May, with 5 half-day in-person PD sessions (one per month). The online component fills the gaps between sessions.**
>
> **Constraint: With 150 teachers ranging from novice to expert, a single track won't work. I'll plan for at least two differentiated tracks: "Foundations" for beginners and "Integration" for experienced teachers.**
>
> **Constraint: Schools with spotty Wi-Fi need offline-capable activities or low-bandwidth alternatives for the online components.**

### AI Solution (Generated from Student's Framing)

**Semester PD Calendar:**

| Month | In-Person Session (Half-Day) | Online Between Sessions |
|-------|------------------------------|------------------------|
| January | Kickoff: PD overview, technology readiness self-assessment, track placement | Week 1-2: Explore assigned tools (video tutorials), complete reflection form |
| February | Workshop: Hands-on tool practice (Track A: Google Workspace basics; Track B: Advanced formative assessment) | Week 1-3: Design one technology-enhanced lesson, share in online forum |
| March | Peer Observation Prep: How to observe and give feedback on tech integration | Week 1-3: Conduct peer observation, upload observation notes |
| April | Data Review: Analyze student engagement data from technology-enhanced lessons | Week 1-3: Revise lesson based on observation feedback, teach revised lesson |
| May | Showcase: Teachers present their best technology-enhanced lesson | Final reflection and program evaluation survey |

**Facilitator Guide (In-Person Sessions):**
- Each session: 30 min large group instruction, 90 min hands-on workshop, 30 min reflection/planning
- Facilitators assigned by track (one per 25 teachers)
- Materials: printed handouts for low-tech participants, shared drive for digital materials

**Online Component Specifications:**
- Platform: Google Classroom (district already uses Google Workspace)
- Content: Pre-recorded video tutorials (5-10 min each), downloadable guides (PDF), discussion forums
- Asynchronous: all activities can be completed anytime within the 3-week window

**Evaluation Plan:**
- Pre/post technology confidence survey
- Lesson plan analysis (rubric scoring the level of technology integration)
- Participant satisfaction survey at end of program

### Framing Feedback

**Criticism:** The student made good practical decisions — defining "technology-enhanced instruction" concretely, planning for two differentiated tracks, and flagging the Wi-Fi constraint. However, the student did not define what competency levels the two tracks target or how teachers are assessed and placed into the right track (self-selection biases toward overconfidence or underconfidence). The student also accepted "see results by spring" without questioning what counts as a "result" — is it teacher satisfaction, knowledge gain, behavior change, or student outcomes? These are fundamentally different things to measure (Kirkpatrick levels). The offline/low-bandwidth concern was mentioned but no specific solution was proposed.

**Better Alternative Framing:**
> "Assumption: 'Technology-enhanced instruction' means specific competencies — I need to define 3-5 target competencies with proficiency levels. Question: How are teachers placed into tracks? Self-assessment alone is unreliable — a skills demonstration or structured inventory would be more valid. Constraint: 'See results by spring' needs decomposition — teacher satisfaction (measurable immediately), knowledge gain (measurable with assessments), behavior change (requires classroom observation), student outcomes (requires a full semester or more). Which does the superintendent mean? Question: For low-bandwidth schools, what specific offline alternatives will be provided — downloadable materials? USB drives? Printed packets?"

**Grade: B** — Good practical instincts, but missed competency definitions, placement validity, and outcome decomposition.

#### Updated AI Output

**Revised Observation Strategy:**

Instead of 300 external observations, use a three-tiered approach:
1. **Peer Observation (all 150 teachers):** Teachers paired cross-school. Each pair conducts 2 mutual observations using a structured ISTE-aligned protocol. Observations focus on the specific competency the teacher is practicing.
2. **Video Self-Review (all 150 teachers):** Teachers record one technology-enhanced lesson (phone on tripod, no production quality needed). They complete a self-analysis worksheet comparing their lesson to the competency rubric.
3. **Facilitator Spot-Check (sample of 30 teachers):** Facilitators observe 30 randomly selected teachers (15 per track) for calibration — this verifies the peer observation data and provides quality assurance for the evaluation.

**Estimated staff time:** 30 observations x 45 min each = 22.5 hours of facilitator observation time across the semester, manageable for 6 facilitators.

**Track Placement Process:**

| Step | Activity | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| 1. Self-Assessment | Online questionnaire (15 items: "I can create a Google Doc" to "I have used real-time polling in class") | Initial screening, takes 10 minutes |
| 2. Skills Check | At January kickoff: 15-minute task — create a 3-question quiz in Google Forms with an answer key | Validates self-assessment against demonstrated skill |
| 3. Placement | Self-assessment + skills check → recommended track. Teachers can accept or request alternative. | Combines self-report with performance data |
| 4. Adjustment Window | After February session: teachers can request track switch with facilitator approval | Catches misplacements early |

#### Judging Feedback
