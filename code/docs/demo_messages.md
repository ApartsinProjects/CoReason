# CoReason Demo Session — Key Messages (Top 50)

> Messages to communicate during a live demo, organized by role and flow.
> Each message is tagged: **[S]** = Student, **[I]** = Instructor, **[A]** = All roles.

## Login & First Impression (Messages 1–8)

1. **[A]** "CoReason is an AI co-reasoning assessment platform — it measures how well students think *with* AI, not just what they know."

2. **[A]** "The platform supports full Hebrew and English interfaces — switch languages instantly from any page."

3. **[A]** "For this demo, we'll use test accounts. Select a user from the dropdown — no password needed."

4. **[A]** "Notice the role badge — Student or Instructor — determines what you see and can do."

5. **[I]** "As an instructor, you land on your challenge management dashboard. Your courses, challenges, and student analytics are all accessible from the top navigation."

6. **[S]** "As a student, you land on the challenge list — challenges from courses you're subscribed to, ready to attempt."

## Student Flow — Course Subscription (Messages 9–14)

9. **[S]** "The Course Catalog shows all courses at your institution. You can filter by department or subscription status."

10. **[S]** "Each course card shows the number of instructors, published challenges, and enrolled students — helping you choose."

11. **[S]** "Subscribe to a course with one click. Unsubscribe just as easily. Subscribed courses appear in your challenge filters."

12. **[S]** "Subject tags on each course card preview the topics covered — these drive the challenge content."

13. **[S]** "The institution name appears at the top — you only see courses from your own institution."

14. **[S]** "After subscribing, click 'View Challenges' to see all published challenges in that course."

## Student Flow — The Challenge Run (Messages 15–27)

15. **[S]** "This is the core experience: a 3-phase co-reasoning session with AI. Phase 1 is Framing, Phase 2 is iterative Judging and Steering cycles."

16. **[S]** "Challenges come in two modes: Practice (unlimited attempts, immediate feedback) and Assessment (graded, limited attempts)."

17. **[S]** "When you start a challenge, the AI generates a unique problem based on the course subjects. No two students get the exact same problem."

18. **[S]** "In Phase 1 (Framing), you see the raw problem and must choose or describe how to approach it — this tests your ability to frame a problem for AI."

19. **[S]** "Multiple-choice mode presents AI-generated options. Open-ended mode lets you write structured sections describing your approach."

20. **[S]** "After you submit your framing, the AI generates its first solution attempt based on your guidance."

21. **[S]** "In Phase 2 (Judging), you evaluate the AI's output. Can you spot what's wrong? What's missing? What's misleading?"

22. **[S]** "Judging tests critical evaluation — a key skill when working with AI systems that can produce confident but incorrect answers."

23. **[S]** "After judging, you Steer — provide corrections and refinements to guide the AI toward a better solution."

24. **[S]** "This Judge-Steer cycle repeats up to the maximum number of cycles configured by the instructor."

25. **[S]** "You can end early by clicking 'Solution is complete' if you're satisfied with the AI's output before reaching the maximum cycles."

26. **[S]** "At the end, you receive separate grades for each skill: Framing (A/B/C), Judging (A/B/C), and Steering (A/B/C)."

27. **[S]** "The session summary panel on the right tracks the evolution: the original problem, current AI solution, and your steering history."

## Student Flow — Analytics & Results (Messages 28–33)

28. **[S]** "My Results shows your performance across all completed challenge runs with visual analytics."

29. **[S]** "Three distribution cards show your grade distribution for each skill — Framing, Judging, and Steering — so you can see your strengths."

30. **[S]** "The trend timeline plots your grades over time — track improvement across multiple challenge attempts."

31. **[S]** "Click any completed run to see the full report: your responses, the AI's outputs, and detailed feedback per phase."

32. **[S]** "Filter by course, challenge type, or grade to focus on specific areas for improvement."

33. **[S]** "Practice challenges can be re-run — use them to improve before attempting the assessment."

---

## Instructor Flow — Course & Subject Management (Messages 34–39)

34. **[I]** "My Courses shows courses where you're assigned as Lead or Co-Instructor, filtered by your institution."

35. **[I]** "Each course has a Subject Tree — a hierarchical knowledge map that drives challenge generation."

36. **[I]** "The Subject Tree Editor supports manual editing, AI-powered generation, and YAML import/export."

37. **[I]** "AI Generate builds a complete subject tree from the course name and description — then you refine it."

38. **[I]** "Each node in the tree can have a description and keywords that improve the quality of generated challenges."

39. **[I]** "Subject trees are shared across all challenges in a course — update the tree, and future challenges reflect the changes."

---

## Instructor Flow — Challenge Creation (Messages 40–44)

40. **[I]** "Create a challenge by selecting a course, choosing subject nodes, and configuring the response type."

41. **[I]** "You can configure Phase 1 (Framing) and Phase 2 (Judging/Steering) independently as multiple-choice or open-ended."

42. **[I]** "Set the maximum number of Judge-Steer cycles — more cycles for complex topics, fewer for quick assessments."

43. **[I]** "The preview panel shows a sample problem and auto-generated rubric as you configure — what you see is what students will get."

44. **[I]** "Mark challenges as Practice (students can retry) or Assessment (graded, results tracked)."

---

## Instructor Flow — Analytics & Monitoring (Messages 45–48)

45. **[I]** "Course Analytics shows aggregated results across all subscribers who completed challenges."

46. **[I]** "Grade distribution charts for each skill (Framing, Judging, Steering) reveal class-wide patterns."

47. **[I]** "The per-student results table lets you drill into individual performance — click Review to see their full session."

48. **[I]** "Export analytics as PDF for reporting or grade integration."

## Big Picture & Pedagogy (Messages 49–50)

49. **[A]** "CoReason aligns with multiple pedagogical frameworks: Bloom's Taxonomy (the 3 phases map to Analyze, Evaluate, Create), Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development (AI as scaffolding), and Schön's Reflective Practice (the iterative Judge-Steer loop)."

50. **[A]** "The platform measures a skill set that traditional assessments miss: the ability to frame problems for AI, critically evaluate AI output, and iteratively refine AI-generated solutions — the core competencies of the AI era."
