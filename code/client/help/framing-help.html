Phase 1 — Framing

What is Framing? Framing is the skill of turning a vague, ill-defined problem into a well-structured specification that an AI can solve effectively.

Why It Matters

AI systems produce much better results when given clear, specific instructions. Framing teaches you to:

  • Identify missing constraints — What assumptions need to be stated explicitly?
  • Define scope — What should and shouldn't be included?
  • Specify requirements — What does a correct solution look like?
  • Choose the right level of detail — Not too vague, not too prescriptive.

How It Works

  1. You'll see a raw problem statement — intentionally vague and incomplete.
  2. Your task: add refinement sections that clarify the problem.
  3. In multiple-choice mode, select all refinements that apply.
  4. In open-ended mode, write your own refinement sections.
  5. After submitting, the AI generates a solution based on your framing.

Grading

Your framing is graded A / B / C based on how well you identified and addressed the gaps in the original problem.
