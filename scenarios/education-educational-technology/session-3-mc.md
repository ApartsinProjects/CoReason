## PHASE 1 — FRAMING

### Raw Problem Presented to Student

> Design a blended professional development program to train 150 K-12 teachers across a school district in "technology-enhanced instruction." The program should run over one semester. Teachers range from "tech-savvy early adopters" to those who "still use overhead projectors." The district has a half-day per month reserved for PD but "teachers say it's never enough time." Some schools have strong Wi-Fi; others are "spotty at best." Deliver a semester-long PD calendar with the blended schedule, a facilitator guide for in-person sessions, design specifications for the online components, and a plan for measuring whether teachers actually change their classroom practice. The superintendent wants to "see results by spring."

### Framing Options Presented

Which assumptions and constraints should you establish before the AI generates a solution?

- **(A)** Assume all teachers need the same training, use only in-person sessions (ignore the blended requirement), and measure success by attendance.
- **(B)** Define "technology-enhanced instruction" as integrating specific digital tools, plan for two differentiated tracks (beginner/advanced), ensure offline alternatives for schools with poor Wi-Fi, and schedule 5 in-person sessions with asynchronous online work between them.
- **(C)** Define "technology-enhanced instruction" as 3-5 specific target competencies with proficiency levels, plan for differentiated tracks with a validated placement process (not just self-selection), decompose "results by spring" into Kirkpatrick levels (satisfaction, learning, behavior, impact) and clarify which the superintendent means, specify concrete offline/low-bandwidth alternatives for every online activity, and question whether 5 half-days is sufficient contact time for the stated outcomes.
- **(D)** Focus only on the online component — in-person PD days are outdated and should be eliminated.
- **(E)** Tell the superintendent that behavior change cannot be measured in one semester, so the program should not attempt evaluation.

**Student selects: (B)**

### AI Solution (Generated from Student's Framing)

**Semester PD Calendar:**

| Month | In-Person (Half-Day) | Online Between Sessions |
|-------|---------------------|------------------------|
| January | Kickoff: Overview, self-assessment, track placement | Video tutorials, reflection form |
| February | Workshop: Hands-on tool practice by track | Design one tech-enhanced lesson, share in forum |
| March | Peer Observation Prep | Conduct peer observation, upload notes |
| April | Data Review: Analyze student engagement data | Revise lesson based on feedback, teach revised lesson |
| May | Showcase: Present best tech-enhanced lesson | Final reflection and program evaluation survey |

**Tracks:** A (Foundations: Google Workspace basics) and B (Integration: Advanced formative assessment)

**Online:** Google Classroom, video tutorials, discussion forums, 3-week completion windows

**Evaluation:** Pre/post confidence survey, lesson plan rubric, satisfaction survey

### Framing Feedback

**Criticism:** Option (B) is practical and addresses several key concerns — differentiated tracks, offline alternatives, and blended scheduling. However, it does not define target competencies (what should teachers be able to DO?), does not question how track placement works (self-selection is biased), and does not decompose "results by spring" into measurable outcome levels. Option (C) was stronger because it specified competencies, placement validity, Kirkpatrick outcome levels, and questioned dosage sufficiency.

**Better Alternative:** Option **(C)** — it includes everything in (B) plus competency specificity, placement validation, outcome decomposition, and dosage questioning.

**Grade: B** — Solid practical plan, but missed pedagogical precision.

**Revised Observation Strategy:**
