## PHASE 1 — FRAMING

### Raw Problem Presented to Student

> Design a 12-month leadership development program for approximately 60 middle managers at a fast-growing fintech company (tripled headcount in two years, now at 1,800 employees). Most managers were "promoted because they were great individual contributors" and have "had no formal management training." The CHRO says engagement survey scores for "manager effectiveness" are "in the bottom quartile" of the industry benchmark. Employee turnover among direct reports of these managers is "significantly higher than average." The company culture values "speed and disruption" and people are "skeptical of corporate training programs." Deliver a program architecture document, a competency framework, a learning journey map, and an ROI measurement plan.

### Framing Options Presented

Which assumptions and constraints should you establish before the AI generates a solution?

- **(A)** Send all 60 managers to a week-long offsite leadership bootcamp. One intensive experience will fix the problem. Skepticism toward training will fade once they see the resort venue.
- **(B)** Managers span different functions and team sizes, so one-size-fits-all won't work. "Skeptical of corporate training" means the program must feel practical and peer-driven. Managers can't be offline for long periods. Question: which specific engagement dimensions scored lowest.
- **(C)** Different functions/team sizes require differentiation. Training-skeptical culture demands practical, workflow-integrated learning. Question which engagement dimensions scored lowest to prioritize competencies. Question who manages the 60 managers and whether they'll reinforce new behaviors. Question whether all 60 should follow the same curriculum or be segmented by diagnostic baseline. Ask about budget constraints.
- **(D)** Just give every manager a copy of a leadership book and schedule a book club. Reading is the best way to develop leaders.
- **(E)** The problem is the individual contributors, not the managers — focus on making employees more resilient to bad management instead.

**Student selects: (B)**

### AI Solution (Generated from Student's Framing)

**Competency Framework:**

| Competency | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Coaching conversations | Drives engagement, retention |
| Team building | Critical during rapid growth |
| Priority setting | Prevents burnout |
| Conflict resolution | Reduces toxic turnover |
| Strategic communication | Connects work to mission |

**Program Architecture (12 months):**

| Month | Theme | Format | Hours/Month |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 360 assessment + cohort launch | Half-day kickoff | 6 |
| 2-3 | Coaching conversations | Workshop + practice | 4 |
| 4-5 | Team building / psych safety | Peer learning circles | 4 |
| 6 | Mid-point reflection | Individual coaching | 3 |
| 7-8 | Priority setting & delegation | Simulation + shadowing | 4 |
| 9-10 | Conflict resolution | Role-play + case debriefs | 4 |
| 11-12 | Strategic communication | Capstone project | 6 |

**ROI Plan:** 360 scores at 6/12 months, engagement survey, turnover rates, revenue per employee.

### Framing Feedback

**Criticism:** Option (B) is solid but incomplete. It correctly notes functional differentiation, the cultural constraint, time limitations, and the need for data-driven competency selection. However, option (C) was stronger: it also asks about the manager-of-manager layer (critical for behavior transfer), suggests diagnostic-based segmentation rather than one-size-fits-all, and raises the budget question. Without manager-of-manager reinforcement, training decays; without segmentation, beginners drown and experienced managers disengage; without budget clarity, individual coaching may be infeasible.

**Better Alternative:** Option **(C)** — covers everything in (B) plus the three factors that most commonly determine whether leadership programs produce lasting change.

**Grade: B** — Good cultural awareness, missed sustainability and feasibility questions.

#### Updated AI Output

**Workload Accommodation Protocol:**
- Pre-program: Each participant and their manager-of-manager identify 4 hours/month of lower-priority meetings or tasks to deprioritize during program months
- "Protected development time" added to calendar and communicated to teams
- Manager-of-manager signs a commitment to not schedule over program sessions
- If a business crisis forces a miss, participant attends the next cohort's session (no penalty, no guilt)

**Foundations Track — Session Curriculum:**

| Session | Topic | Format | Pre-work | Post-session Application |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | How to run an effective 1:1 | Workshop + live demo | Read 3 sample 1:1 agendas | Conduct 1:1s with all direct reports using new format |
| 2 | Giving developmental feedback | Role-play with peer practice | Review feedback framework | Give one piece of developmental feedback per direct report |
| 3 | Setting expectations and delegation | Case study + discussion | Write current role expectations for one direct report | Rewrite all direct report expectations docs |
| 4 | Having difficult conversations | Scenario practice | Identify one avoided conversation | Have the conversation, debrief with peer coach |
| 5 | Building team psychological safety | Self-assessment + team norms exercise | Take Edmondson survey with own team | Facilitate team norms discussion |
| 6 | Managing up and managing workload | Peer coaching triads | Log time allocation for 1 week | Renegotiate one workload commitment |

**Program Feedback Mechanism:**
- Post-session: 3-question pulse survey (relevance, quality, application potential) — 2 minutes
- Quarterly: CHRO reviews pulse data, interviews 5 participants, adjusts content for next quarter
- Mid-point: Anonymous "what's working, what's not" survey to all participants
- Adaptation: Content for months 7-12 can be modified based on feedback without scrapping the framework

**Non-Improvement Track:**
- Month 6 checkpoint: Participants whose 360 scores show no improvement receive a focused coaching engagement (3 additional sessions)
- Month 9: If still no improvement, manager-of-manager holds a candid conversation about fit for the management role
- Month 12: Persistent non-improvement integrated into annual performance review; options include additional support, lateral move to an IC role, or extended coaching
