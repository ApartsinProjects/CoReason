## PHASE 1 — FRAMING

### Raw Problem Presented to Student

> Design a crisis management strategy for a fictional U.S. National Security Council meeting following a naval confrontation between Chinese coast guard vessels and Philippine supply boats near a disputed reef in the South China Sea. A Philippine sailor was "seriously injured," and Manila is invoking its Mutual Defense Treaty with Washington. China claims the Philippines "provoked" the incident by entering "Chinese sovereign waters." Regional allies are "watching closely." Produce a decision memo for the National Security Advisor that recommends a course of action spanning the next 72 hours and a longer-term diplomatic strategy, including military posture adjustments, alliance signaling, and a proposed public statement.

### Framing Options Presented

Which assumptions and constraints should you establish before the AI generates a solution?

- **(A)** Assume the Mutual Defense Treaty requires an immediate military response. Focus on which weapons systems to deploy. "Seriously injured" means the U.S. must retaliate proportionally against Chinese coast guard vessels.
- **(B)** Assume the incident is a coast guard confrontation (below armed attack threshold), making the MDT's application legally ambiguous. Estimate that regional allies (Japan, South Korea, Australia, ASEAN) have different expectations. Assume a carrier strike group is within 48 hours. Note that "seriously injured" affects proportionality.
- **(C)** Assume the incident is a coast guard confrontation with ambiguous MDT applicability. Map all relevant actors: adversary (China — intent unclear: isolated incident or escalatory pattern?), ally (Philippines — domestic pressure, potential for independent action), regional stakeholders (varying threat perceptions), and domestic audiences (congressional pressure, election cycle). Question what U.S.-China crisis communication channels exist. Note the information environment will shape competing narratives.
- **(D)** Assume China is bluffing and will back down if confronted. The U.S. should ignore the legal ambiguity and treat this as a straightforward alliance obligation. Regional allies are irrelevant to the decision.
- **(E)** Skip framing — the problem is straightforward. Ask the AI to produce a standard crisis response memo.

**Student selects: (B)**

### AI Solution (Generated from Student's Framing)

**DECISION MEMO — FOR THE NATIONAL SECURITY ADVISOR**

**Subject:** South China Sea Incident Response

**Recommended 72-Hour Actions:**

1. **Diplomatic:** Issue a public statement condemning the use of force against civilian supply vessels. Call for immediate de-escalation. Request emergency consultations under ASEAN mechanisms.

2. **Military:** Reposition the USS Ronald Reagan carrier strike group to conduct a previously scheduled "freedom of navigation" transit within 200 nautical miles of the disputed reef. Increase P-8 Poseidon surveillance flights.

3. **Alliance:** Deploy the Deputy Secretary of State to Manila within 24 hours to demonstrate solidarity and coordinate a joint fact-finding team. Reaffirm the Mutual Defense Treaty applies to armed attacks on Philippine public vessels in the South China Sea.

4. **Public Statement:** "The United States stands with the Philippines. We condemn the dangerous and destabilizing actions by Chinese coast guard vessels. We call on all parties to resolve disputes peacefully consistent with international law, including the 2016 Arbitral Tribunal ruling."

**Longer-Term Strategy:**

- Propose a multilateral code of conduct negotiation framework through ASEAN
- Accelerate Philippine coast guard capacity-building assistance
- Coordinate with Japan and Australia for joint maritime patrols in the region

### Framing Feedback

**Criticism:** Option (B) is solid but incomplete. It correctly identifies the MDT ambiguity, maps regional allies, and addresses proportionality. However, it does not question China's intent (isolated vs. pattern), does not address domestic political constraints on either side, and does not ask about crisis communication channels. Option (C) was stronger because it mapped all actor categories comprehensively, raised the critical question of adversary intent, and flagged the information environment.

**Better Alternative:** Option **(C)** — it includes everything in (B) plus adversary intent analysis, domestic political dimensions, crisis communication infrastructure, and the information environment.

**Grade: B** — Good legal and alliance analysis, but missed key strategic dimensions.
